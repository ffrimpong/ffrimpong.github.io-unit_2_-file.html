Why First Round NFL Picks Usually Come From The Same Schools

By: Fiifi Frimpong

The current top five teams in college football rankings, Louisiana State University, Ohio State, Clemson, Georgia and Alabama, are teams filled with talented prospects primed to have their names called in the first round of the NFL draft. Each April, Roger Goodell, the NFL commissioner, announces first round picks that teams select and fans watch them play each Sunday. But how are these schools able to recruit, recycle these talented first round prospects every year and stay relevant to prospects coming out of high school?

Unlike other college sports, the powerhouse college football programs are able to land first round talent by consistently winning a majority of their games. Strong recruiting translates into wins and wins translates into strong recruiting.
That is why Alabama, Ohio St. and Clemson are at the top of the list in the past three seasons when it comes to total wins and first round picks.
